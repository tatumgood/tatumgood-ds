Featured Projects

The Odds of Participation: Socioeconomic Predictors of Sports-Gambling Activity and Its Implications

   

This research project examines how education and income levels relate to sports betting participation across U.S. states. Using data from the U.S. Census and state-level sports betting reports (2018–2023), the study explores whether modern app-based gambling follows traditional socioeconomic patterns. Built with Docker, Supabase, and PostgreSQL, the pipeline supported statistical modeling and visualization through linear regression, random forests, and choropleth mapping. Findings reveal that higher education and income levels are associated with greater sports betting activity, suggesting that participation is driven more by access and disposable income than by financial distress.

New York Times Data Visualization

 

This graduate research project analyzes 173 New York Times graphics from 2015–2024 to explore how data visualization practices changed during the COVID-19 pandemic. Using qualitative coding and R-based analysis, the study examines shifts in graphic design, complexity, and communication themes to understand how visuals both reflected and influenced public perception during the crisis.

Metro Pipeline

     

This project delivers an end-to-end data pipeline for analyzing public transportation ridership and fare data. The system ingests, processes, and visualizes daily rider behavior and fare usage trends using Airflow, PostgreSQL, and Grafana. Our goal was to support data-driven decisions around fare policy, rider equity, and system performance.

Student Performance Factors

   

As team leader, I guided the group through a project analyzing factors that influence student exam performance using a synthetic dataset of over 6,600 records. The work involved exploratory data analysis and the development of predictive models to investigate relationships between sleep, study habits, motivation, and socioeconomic status. Feature engineering and data visualizations were used to identify traits associated with an “ideal” student profile.
