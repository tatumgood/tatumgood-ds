Overview

This project investigates the multifaceted nature of academic performance and what contributes to the idea of an “ideal” student. A literature review of recent studies revealed that there is no single definition of an ideal student, but rather a spectrum of traits—ranging from motivation and ambition to community engagement and socioeconomic support. Using a synthetic dataset from Kaggle titled StudentPerformanceFactors.csv, which includes 6,607 observations on variables such as study habits, attendance, sleep, parental involvement, and socioeconomic background, the project aimed to identify the most influential factors in predicting academic performance. The analysis combined exploratory data techniques, statistical correlation tests, regression models, and predictive machine learning to investigate student outcomes and answer core research questions.

Skills and Tools Used

A comprehensive toolkit for data exploration, visualization, and reporting in R. We also focused on qualitative coding of design principles and topics. Namely, data visualization theory and keeping track of factors such as Gestalt principles and perception tasks to see trends arise. It was also beneficial to engage in a literature review synthesis and academic writing.
