Overview

This project delivers an end-to-end data pipeline for analyzing public transportation ridership and fare data. The system ingests, processes, and visualizes daily rider behavior and fare usage trends using Airflow, PostgreSQL, and Grafana. Our goal was to support data-driven decisions around fare policy, rider equity, and system performance.

Skills and Tools Used

A comprehensive toolkit for data exploration, visualization, and reporting in R. We also focused on qualitative coding of design principles and topics. Namely, data visualization theory and keeping track of factors such as Gestalt principles and perception tasks to see trends arise. It was also beneficial to engage in a literature review synthesis and academic writing.

Data Process

Stat Nuggets

   

We began by collecting all available “What’s Going On in This Graph?” articles published by the New York Times Learning Network in collaboration with the American Statistical Association. These weekly publications were designed to promote visual literacy in classrooms, but they also provide a consistent, publicly accessible record of data visualizations in media from 2015–2024.

Data Collection and Cleaning

   

After compiling 173 articles, we manually coded metadata for each graphic: type (bar, line, scatter, etc.), topic (e.g., health, economy, sports), presence of interactivity, design complexity, and use of Gestalt principles. Each graphic was also analyzed for its interpretive demands—such as number of perception tasks required and whether uncertainty (e.g., confidence intervals) was visually communicated.
